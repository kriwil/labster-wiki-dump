The catalytic constant (kcat) or turnover number is the number of enzymatic reactions a single saturated enzyme molecule can catalyze per unit of time, usually expressed in s-1. kcat is not dependent on the concentration of the enzyme, and is therefore a better parameter than Vmax for comparing different enzymes. Because kcat is the maximum number of chemical reactions a single enzyme molecule can catalyze, the maximum velocity (Vmax) at a specific enzyme concentration is obtained by multiplying kcat with the concentration of the enzyme ([E]), i.e., Vmax = kcat • [E].
