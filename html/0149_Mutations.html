Mutations are changes in the nucleotide sequence of the DNA as compared with a wild type sequence.


Mutations are categorized according to how they change the order of bases:

Substitution: one base is replaced by another

Deletion: one or more bases are deleted

Insertions: one or more bases are inserted

(Chapter 7 page 200)


Mutations in coding sequences are categorized according to their effect on the protein:

Synonymous/silent mutation: a substitution that does not change the amino acid

Non-synonymous/missense mutation: a substitution that changes the amino acid

Nonsense mutation: generates a stop codon

Frameshift mutation: insertion or deletion not divisible by 3 that alters the reading frame

(Chapter 8 page 276)
